The Crocodile by Lewis Carroll
How doth the little crocodile
Improve his shining tail,
And pour the waters of the Nile
On every golden scale!

How cheerfully he seems to grin,
How neatly spreads his claws,
And welcomes little fishes in,
With gently smiling jaws!
Analysis:

This poem is a playful satire of Isaac Watts' moralistic poem, "Against Idleness and Mischief." Lewis Carroll uses irony to depict a crocodile that appears cheerful while being predatory.

Understanding Grammar Rules
  • Nouns: Words that name people, places, or things.
  • Verbs: Words that express action or a state of being.
  • Adjectives: Words that describe nouns.
Common Grammar Mistakes:
  • Its vs. It’s: "Its" is possessive, while "it’s" means "it is".
  • There, Their, They’re: Different uses based on meaning.
